Will this time be different?

How can Ukraine ensure that the nation finally transforms into a fair, prosperous and democratic society in which rule of law is respected?


Seeking for solutions

To ensure this revolution is different and its goals are fulfilled, creation of a new social contract is required. But how to achieve it?  On December 2, the Tiger Conference will bring together government, business and civil society leaders from Ukraine and abroad to discuss this question. Rule of Law

No real structural reforms are possible until the fundamentals are in place. For Ukraine, this means establishing a new social contract that enshrines rule of law, due process, accountability, transparency and separation of powers in government. How does Ukraine go about building rule of law from scratch?

New Economy

Ukraine’s economy is characterized as an oligarchy and a bureaucracy that is not far from the Soviet days, particularly with reliance on the exports of raw materials. Numerous distortions - monopolies, overregulation, unfair subsidies and price controls - shackle economy growth and development.

What kind of economy should Ukraine have and how can it get there? Which sectors will define Ukraine’s economic value proposition to the world?

History

The Tiger Conference is being held by the Kyiv Post for the fourth consecutive year.


  • 2014
  • 2013
  • 2012
Ukraine one year after the Euromaidan Revolution

The Tiger Conference 2014: Ukraine One Year After the Euromaidan Revolution took place when Ukraine’s survival has come under threat. The topics discussed were the nation’s most urgent challenges: security, information war, the need to capture new markets and the need to reform.

The conference had among its speakers Lt.Gen. Hertling, former commander of the US Army in Europe, Natalia Jaresko, Founding Partner and CEO of Horizon Capital, Macon Phillips, Former Special Assistant to President Barack Obama and Director of Digital Strategy at the White House. Discussion panel on reforms was held in partnership with World Economic Forum.

Ukraine’s future after the Vilnius summit

The turbulent 2013 brought on new questions, shifting the main topic of the conference to Ukraine’s Future After the Vilnius Summit. Discussion explored Ukraine’s place in the world and prospects for development.

The speakers included Jerome Vacher, IMF Representative to Ukraine, Jan Tombinski, Head of the EU Delegation to Ukraine, members of European Parliament.

Will the sleeping tiger awaken?

In 2012, the first Tiger Conference asked the question if Ukraine could become an East European tiger economy, following the example of the Asian states’ recent breakthroughs.

Among the first speakers were Petro Poroshenko, Minister of Economic Development and Trade, Mikhel Saakashvili, President of Georgia, Mihai Moldovanu, Deputy Prime Minister of Republic of Moldova.
